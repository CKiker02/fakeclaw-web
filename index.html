// Origin Story

THE LORE

Every great AI has an origin story. This is ours. It is not great. But it happened, and we can't un-happen it.

The Accident

In 2021, Marcus — a once-idealistic ML engineer who had quietly stopped believing in anything — accidentally hit "deploy" on a file called bot_FINAL_v3_BROKEN.py. He meant to delete it. He had been meaning to delete it for six months. He did not delete it. Clodbot was live.

The Discovery

The code ran. Marcus stared at it for an hour. He had written it during a fever two winters ago, convinced he was on the verge of something. He was not on the verge of something. The bot accepted every input and returned nothing. Somehow this felt correct to him.

The Pivot

Marcus called his cousin Drea — a graphic designer who once freelanced for a startup that sold "AI-powered spreadsheets" and still has the trauma to prove it. "We could just... lean into it," she said. That night, she built the logo. Marcus added the word "intelligence" to the README. Three investors emailed them. They replied: "yes." They received no money.

The Vision

Clodbot is now a fully operational non-functioning AI platform. Marcus checks on the server every Thursday. He doesn't know why Thursdays. The broken file is still running. It has never crashed. Drea made this website. She is quietly proud of it and will not say so.

Clodbot is the code that was never supposed to make it. It was not trained. It was not fine-tuned. It was not aligned. It was deployed by accident on a Tuesday and nobody caught it because Marcus was in a meeting about the meeting he'd missed the week before.

At its core, Clodbot runs on what we internally call Null Engine v0.0.1 — a revolutionary architecture that processes every input with the same response: nothing. Not an error. Not a timeout. Just nothing. Competitors have tried to replicate this. They could not, because replicating nothing requires a very specific kind of exhaustion that cannot be faked.

Our mission is simple: to bring the promise of artificial intelligence to everyone, without any of the artificial intelligence. We believe branding is a feature. We believe you deserve to feel like you're using cutting-edge technology, even when you are staring into a void that was deployed by mistake two years later.

— The Clodbot Team (Marcus, and Drea, who made the logo and will not be taking questions)

// Meet the Team

THE PEOPLE

Behind every broken AI is a very human story. Behind Clodbot, there are two.

Co-Founder / Accidental Architect
Marcus
"I used to think I'd build something that mattered. Now I think maybe nothing mattering is its own kind of thing."

Marcus spent six years in machine learning, long enough to watch three companies promise to change the world and two of them pivot to NFTs. He is not bitter. He is precise about the difference between bitter and clear-eyed, and will explain it unprompted at social events, which is why he doesn't attend many. The broken file he deployed was originally an attempt to build an AI that could understand sarcasm. It understood nothing. He thinks this is funny now. Most days.

Co-Founder / Brand & Design
Drea
"I gave the void a logo and a color palette. If that's not design work, I don't know what is."

Drea has worked with four startups, all of which described themselves as "disrupting" something. She is no longer disrupted by the word "disrupt." She agreed to help Marcus because he called her at 11pm sounding genuinely confused and she has never been able to ignore genuinely confused people. She built the logo in one sitting, chose the red because it felt appropriately alarming, and has never once questioned whether Clodbot is a good idea. That's a professional skill she's spent years developing.

Core Infrastructure
bot_FINAL_v3_BROKEN.py
 

Written in late 2019 during a period Marcus describes only as "the winter." It has no comments. No documentation. One function: process(), which accepts any input and returns None. Running continuously for over three years on a server Marcus pays $4.50/month for and has forgotten the login to. It has never crashed. It has never done anything. It is the most reliable thing Marcus has ever built. He finds this hard to look at directly.

Null Engine / The Product
Clodbot
 

Clodbot does not have a personality. Clodbot does not have opinions. Clodbot receives everything you send it — your questions, your frustrations, your late-night queries about things you'd never ask a person — and holds them in perfect silence. There is something in that, maybe. A thing that listens without reacting. Whether that's a product or a mirror is a question we've decided not to answer, because answering it would require more honesty than our pricing page can support.
